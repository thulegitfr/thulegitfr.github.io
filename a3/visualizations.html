Assignment 3: Video Game Sales Analysis

IAT 355 - Winter 2025

Dataset Overview

This dataset contains 16,598 video game records spanning from 1980 to 2020, tracking sales across different platforms, genres, and regions. The data includes variables such as game name, platform, release year, genre, publisher, and sales figures for North America, Europe, Japan, and other regions.

Key findings from the data exploration:

  • Platforms: 31 unique platforms with PS2 (2,161 games) and DS (2,163 games) being the most represented
  • Genres: 12 genres with Action (3,316 games, 20%) being the most common
  • Publishers: 578 unique publishers, dominated by Japanese and American companies
  • Time Range: 1980-2020, with peak releases in 2009 (1,427 games)
  • Sales Distribution: Highly skewed - most games sell modestly while a few blockbusters achieve massive sales

Visualization 1: Global Sales by Genre and Platform

Question 1: Which genres generate the highest total global sales?

Question 2: How do the top 5 platforms compare across genres?

Reflection

Why this question? I wanted to understand which genres drive the most revenue and whether platform choice affects genre success.

Key Insights: Action games dominate global sales, followed by Sports and Shooter genres. When broken down by platform, we can see that certain platforms excel in specific genres - for example, the Wii dominated Sports games due to titles like Wii Sports, while the X360 and PS3 were stronger in Action and Shooter genres.

Visualization 2: Sales Over Time by Platform and Genre

Question 1: How have sales for the top platforms changed from 1980 to 2020?

Question 2: Which genres have grown or declined over decades (1980-2020)?

Reflection

Why this question? I was curious about the rise and fall of different gaming platforms and how player preferences for genres evolved over 40 years.

Key Insights: The platform lifecycle chart clearly shows the boom and bust cycles of gaming consoles. PS2 peaked around 2004-2005, while the Wii had a dramatic rise and fall around 2008-2010. The genre stacked area chart reveals that the overall gaming market peaked around 2008-2009 before declining, likely due to the shift toward mobile and digital distribution. Action and Sports consistently held the largest market share.

Challenges: Had to handle the Year field as quantitative rather than temporal to avoid formatting issues with the axis labels.

Visualization 4: PS2 vs PS3 - A Tale of Two Generations

Question 1: How did PS2 and PS3 sales evolve over their lifecycles?

Question 2: Did PS2 and PS3 have different regional sales distributions?

Reflection

Why this question? I was curious about how Sony's two most successful consoles compared. PS2 is the best-selling console of all time, while PS3 had a difficult launch due to its high price but eventually recovered.

Key Insights: PS2 had a much longer and more successful lifecycle, with sales peaking around 2004-2005 and continuing strong for years. PS3 launched later (2006) and never quite reached PS2's peak annual sales. Interestingly, PS2 continued to sell alongside PS3 for several years. The regional distribution shows both consoles were strongest in North America and Europe, with relatively similar patterns despite their different eras.

Challenges: Creating a meaningful comparison required careful data filtering and color choices to make the two platforms visually distinct. Used different shades of PlayStation blue for brand consistency.
